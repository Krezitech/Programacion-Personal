DELOREAN DISPUTE PUTS FOCUS ON TOWN IN UTAH

By Judith Cummings

Sept. 6, 1984

You could say that Logan, a town tucked into a mountain valley in northern Utah, was off the beaten track.

Residents say the most famous person to come from here is probably Merlin Olsen, the football player turned sports commentator and actor. When people in Logan talk about going to Paris, they often mean Paris, Idaho, about 40 miles northeast of here.

Yet, somewhere in the halls of Westminster Palace, where the British Parliament meets, there are men and women in high places whose thoughts are occasionally directed to this dairy town of 25,000 people.

A dispute has pitted John Z. DeLorean, chairman of the bankrupt DeLorean Motor Company, against onetime investors and creditors, including the British Government, in a battle over who owns a Logan-based company. The object of this attention is the Logan Manufacturing Company, a 22-acre factory near the outskirts of town that makes vehicles to groom ski slopes. Creditors Seek Logan Company
